Course Comments

[Course Code - Coordinator]

Fall 2023

mat102 - Marina Tvalavadze

Easiest course I have taken, since she had been coordinating 102 for many times (and liying as the TA coordinator too), the questions on the quizzes / final exam are very similar to the ones she gave previuosly. She also leaks literally all homework solutions and the questions topics in OH / on Quercus, so basically you do one or two questions from the textbook relating to those topics then you can get a free 97 (not 100 because of the in-class Mathmatize polls ;-;)

isp100 - Mark Blaauw-Hara

Very friendly professor, his classes are very engaging and exciting, but my class was too shy / not willing to participate, but I really enjoyed the lectures (even though I was working on 108 labs throughout the classes…)

csc108 - Michael Liut

I feel like Liut’s 108 scared alot of people away from CS, his questions on the tests are tough enough to let people with decent coding experiences finish them on time, the decrypt question was so painful… Overall, because he agreed to change the second term test mark to be same as the final (if higher), I would say the course wasn’t too bad, especially it is “introduction to computer programming”.

mat157 - Qun Wang

Truly the best teacher I have ever had. Despite he is probably too busy on other stuff (so the homework and lecture notes are often not being released on time), his lectures and office hours are so amazing, we can feel that he knows all the topics and knows how to connect them in a clear and precise way. His content is really different compared to Tyler’s 157 or UTSG’s 157, he followed the Russian introductory analysis books Zorich 卓里奇, and used the problem set book 吉米多维奇 for most of the homework / term tests / final exam questions. I really think the broadness of Zorich is much better compared to Spivak, especially qun’s Zorich.

Winter 2024

mat240 - Jaimal Thind

Maybe because I never attend lectures and focus on tutorials, I can’t see why people likes Jaimal that much at least from his book (kinda unintuitive to me, compared to like Friedberg and other books), this is probably the easiest course of this sem and I did worst on this course… Because of both too overconfident and too computational I guess (true and false questions worth 40% for the final, definitions worth like 10% for the final, I kinda hate how the final was so computational).

csc240 - Faith Ellen

The course content is really really fun, I enjoyed solving hard math problems, enjoyed learning how to solve recurrences, and enjoyed how to construct automata (especially the parallel ones). Her assignment questions were so tough, and this year she made the mid term and final to be extremely hard compared to most of the previous years, the average for the mid term was 46.81% and most of the people finished like below 65% of the final exam questions (there were 2 hard automata questions, and the program correctness question was so long, it worth like half of the entire final exam). Her lectures are jumping very fast, and she often can’t really explain clearly what the concepts are, definitely my personal bias (because I write notes during lectures so I can’t really focus that much) but she seems to have a lot typos, and often just copy from her notes… This course is for people who can self study… Or have gifted friends who can give you ideas to solve related questions…

csc148 - Andrew Petersen

Petersen’s lectures are engaging, he knows how to explain the materials, and since he focuses particularly on education, he knows how to let his students learn. I really like his term tests and final exam, the questions are not too complicated, they are like “if you know the ideas, you get the marks”, the coding questions were the simplest ones he could give, and most of the theoretical questions are fundamental / unambiguous.

mat159 - Qun Wang

Amazing course same as 157, because this is his first time teaching 159 and he had some family trouble, he updated homework and lectures notes very slowly, but the lectures are even more amazing, this sem he did not purely give questions from the problem set book, instead he gave so many advanced topics questions such as Quasi-uniform, Pendulum related integrals, elliptic integrals, and so many interesting integrals throughout the homework, and so many interesting questions on the final exam review / final exam. My favorite part is definitely the euler substitution part, he explained from the geometric perspective which was very intuitive and straightforward, so good.
